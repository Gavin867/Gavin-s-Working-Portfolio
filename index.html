Hi, I'm Gavin.

Welcome to my portfolio page.

I enjoy critical thinking and solving problems. The Shunryu Suzuki quote on the image banner has been a part of my internal lexicon for several years, but at the moment it really speaks to me on two levels:

  1. (1) The Roshi is referring to the beginner’s mind as advantageous to maintain when approaching both the familiar and unfamiliar.
  2. (2) In terms of software development, I am in fact a beginner -which makes maintaining a beginner’s mind all the easier for me.

Dry humor aside, the notion of beginner's mind is not a concept purported by Zen practicioners only. Variations of this idea exist in other cultures. Having studied some philosophy myself, I draw a connection to the Socratic paraphrase, "The only true wisdom is in knowing that you know nothing." What both of these similar notions empashize is the important nature humility, honesty, and consistent curiosity play in attaining wisdom -and by extension, becoming an expert in one's craft. The more we learn, the more we appreciate how much we do not yet know.

As I search for my first opportunity in the technology sector, I remain thankful for what I have already experienced and for the future challenges which will facilitate my growth as a developer. I remind myself that no matter how far I go...

...I will always remain 'a beginner' at heart.
